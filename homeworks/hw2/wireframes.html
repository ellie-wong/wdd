  • What is the goal of the website? Who is it intended for? How does the design accomplish this? Write 2-3 sentences answering these questions. (Use the 'p' tag again!)

    Goodreads is a social media website for people who love books. It is used by readers and authors alike to track, rate, review, and recommend books. It also allows authors to communicate directly with their fans, often answering questions about their writing process or hosting giveaway lotteries. The design allows for users to create their own virtual sorted "bookshelves" for others to see-- these include books marked as "want to read", "currently reading", and "read", but the user can also add more custom bookshelves, for example books sorted by genre or for a specific class project. Goodreads also has its own recommendation algorithm based on genres a reader enjoys or books that "similar readers" have rated highly.

  • Write 2-3 sentences about what problems your redesign addressed, and how it solved them.

    Goodreads' website design is extremeley outdated and this is a sentiment broadly shared by its users. The homepage is redundant and cluttered, with the center of focus being an advertisement for an upcoming release the user may or may not be interested in. In addition, the same featured blog post that Goodreads wishes you to read is advertised in three different places on the homepage: on the very top, in the middle, and in the right column. This is a redundant waste of space-- especially if a given user is not interested.

    Thus, to address some of these issues, in my redesiged wireframe of the Goodreads homepage I included the featured post only once, in a carousel at the focus of the page, which would also contain a list of trending books on the site and other articles/lists of interest. I also included a column on the left which contains an activity log for a user and their friends, which would be expandable if clicked or hovered upon. There is really no reason to make the updates feed the main content of the website, especially if, say, one uses Goodreads to mainly keep track of their own reading.
